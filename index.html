Video-Consistent Synthetic Skiing Trajectories

Abstract

Annotated skier trajectories are essential for developing video-based methods for tracking and behavior analysis on ski slopes, but obtaining such data is costly and labor-intensive. In this work, we proposed a framework for generating synthetic skier trajectories using an extended cellular automaton model enriched with skier-specific behavior and local multi-skier anticipation. The simulated trajectories were embedded in a 3D slope representation and projected into 2D image space through a virtual pinhole camera model, enabling video-consistent synthetic observations from multiple viewpoints. We evaluated the proposed approach through behavioral analysis, low-level trajectory distribution comparison with the Slope-Track dataset, and multi-object tracking experiments using DeepEIoU [7] with GlideTrack [3]. The results showed that the generated trajectories reproduced the dominant motion characteristics of real skier trajectories and that incorporating synthetic data during training improved tracking association performance on real ski-slope sequences. These findings suggest that behavior-aware synthetic trajectory generation can provide a useful complement to manually annotated ski-slope video datasets.
